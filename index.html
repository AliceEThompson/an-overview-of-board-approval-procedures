Quiz Question 1 of 3
What must happen before a motion can be debated and voted on by the board?
A The board chair must approve the motion in writing
B Another board member must second the motion
C A majority of members must agree to hear it
D The motion must be submitted 48 hours in advance
✓ Correct! Under Robert's Rules of Order, a motion must be seconded by another board member before it can move to discussion and a vote. The person seconding doesn't have to support the motion — they simply agree it's worthy of consideration.
✗ Not quite. A motion must be seconded by another board member before it can proceed. Without a second, the board chair declares the motion dead for lack of a second.
Quiz Question 2 of 3
Which type of vote requires a higher threshold than 50% — such as 75% — to pass a motion?
A Majority vote
B Plurality vote
C Supermajority vote
D Unanimous consent
✓ Correct! A supermajority vote requires a specified percentage higher than 50% — such as two-thirds or 75% — and is typically reserved for significant board decisions.
✗ Not quite. A supermajority vote requires a higher threshold than a simple majority, such as 75%. Unanimous consent requires all members to agree, while a majority only requires more than 50%.
Quiz Question 3 of 3
In Diligent Boards, which approval type would you use to capture an official signature on finalized meeting minutes?
A Signature approval
B Custom approval
C Proxy approval
D Resolution approval
✓ Correct! Signature approvals in Diligent Boards are used when an official signature is required, such as on finalized meeting minutes or resolutions between meetings.
✗ Not quite. Signature approvals are the correct choice when an official signature is needed. Custom approvals are for decisions that need formal documentation but don't require a digital signature.
